Full Explanation — Lesson 2

Happy feelings, 너무, weather & casual slang

📖 Introduction

This lesson covers how to say you’re so happy, praise someone as too perfect, talk about weather, and use casual slang from real chat messages.

The core happy pattern:

나+너무+기뻐

나 너무 기뻐 = I’m so happy

For “the most in the world” add 세상에서 제일:

나 세상에서 제일 기뻐

I’m the happiest in the world

💬 Happy & Perfect

I'm so happy. 나 너무 기뻐 Jestem taka szczęśliwa.
I'm the happiest in the world. 나 세상에서 제일 기뻐 Jestem najszczęśliwsza na świecie.
You are so perfect. 너 너무 완벽해 Jesteś taka idealna / zbyt idealna.
Stretched for emphasis (casual) 너 너어어어어어무 완벽하잖아 — “You’re sooooo perfect, you know!” · stretching 너무 shows extra feeling · 하잖아 = you know / right?

🌡️ Weather & 너무

너무 means too or so much. With weather adjectives you don’t need a subject — context is enough:

KoreanEnglishPolish
너무 더워It's too hot.Jest zbyt gorąco.
너무 추워It's too cold.Jest zbyt zimno.
너무 핫해too hot (Konglish)zbyt gorąco (anglicyzm)

핫해 borrows English “hot” — common in casual speech among friends.

💬 Slang — ~ 같네

~ 같네 literally means “is like…” — used casually to complain that something is awful:

The weather is awful. 날씨 거지 같네 Pogoda jest okropna. (lit. weather is like a beggar)
My neighbor is awful. 내 이웃 그지 같네 Mój sąsiad jest okropny. (그지 = casual spelling of 거지)
Awful / like a dog (strong slang) 개 같네 Okropne — very informal!
