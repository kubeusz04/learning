Happy, 너무 & Weather Slang

Say how happy you are, praise someone as too perfect, and complain about the weather

1 · Happy — 나 너무 기뻐

How happy you feel

나+너무+기뻐

기뻐 = happy (informal) · 나 너무 기뻐 = I’m so happy

→
기뻐 — happy
→
나 너무 기뻐 — I’m so happy
→
나 세상에서 제일 기뻐 — I’m the happiest in the world

2 · 너무 — too / so much

Use 너무 before an adjective or verb to say too or so.

너+너무+완벽해

너 너무 완벽해 = You are so perfect / too perfect

In casual speech you can stretch the vowels for emphasis: 너 너어어어어어무 완벽하잖아 — “You’re sooooo perfect, you know!” (하잖아 = you know / right?)

3 · Weather — hot, cold & Konglish

너무 더워
It’s too hot
너무 추워
It’s too cold
너무 핫해
too hot (Konglish)

4 · Slang — ~ 같네 (“is like…”)

Casual way to say something is awful — literally “is like a …”

→
날씨 거지 같네 — The weather is awful (lit. like a beggar)
→
내 이웃 그지 같네 — My neighbor is awful
→
개 같네 — awful / “like a dog” (strong slang)

거지 = beggar · 이웃 = neighbor · 개 = dog
