Full Explanation — Lesson 3

Hugs · nicknames · love phrases · człowiek vs ludzie

🤗 1 · A hug — przytulenie

The standard noun for “hug” is przytulenie. In casual speech — especially after chcę — people often say przytulasa.

przytulenie = a hug
Chcę przytulasa. = I want a hug. (casual · from class with Kuba)
Spelling Write Chcę with ę — not Chce.

💭 2 · I want — chcę

Chcę = I want. After it you often use a casual form: przytulasa.

I want a hug. Chcę przytulasa.

👉 3 · Hug me! — imperative

To tell someone “hug me”, use the command form of przytulać + mnie (me).

Hug me. Przytul mnie. Przytul = hug! (to one person)

💛 6 · Sweet nicknames — vocative

Polish has a special form when you call someone — the vocative. These three are very common between close people.

Tip Use them like English “honey” or “sweetie” — warm, not formal. Often with an exclamation: Skarbie, przytul mnie!

👥 8 · Człowiek vs ludzie

Both mean “human(s)”, but they work differently in Polish grammar.

człowiek singular — one person / a human To dobry człowiek. That's a good person.
ludzie plural only — people Ci ludzie są mili. These people are nice.
człowiekludzie
Numbersingular ✓plural only ✓
Meaninga person / human beingpeople (group)
ExampleJeden człowiekWielu ludzi
Englisha person / man (human)people
Korean사람 (한 명)사람들
Remember There is no singular of ludzie — for one person always use człowiek (or informal osoba).
